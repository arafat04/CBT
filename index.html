Cognitive Distortions — Definitions

1. All-or-Nothing Thinking
You see things in black-and-white categories. If your performance falls short of perfect, you see yourself as a total failure.

2. Overgeneralization
You see a single negative event as a never-ending pattern of defeat.

3. Mental Filter
You focus on one negative detail so intensely that your view of reality becomes darkened.

4. Disqualifying the Positive
You reject positive experiences by insisting they “don’t count.”

5. Jumping to Conclusions
You make negative interpretations without evidence.
Mind Reading: Assuming others are reacting negatively.
Fortune Teller Error: Predicting a negative outcome as fact.

6. Magnification (Catastrophizing) or Minimization
You exaggerate problems or shrink positive qualities.

7. Emotional Reasoning
You believe that because you feel something, it must be true.

8. Should Statements
You motivate with “shoulds,” “musts,” and “oughts,” leading to guilt or anger.

9. Labeling and Mislabeling
You attach global negative labels instead of describing specific behavior.

10. Personalization
You assume responsibility for negative events beyond your control.
